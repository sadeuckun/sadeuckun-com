Accessibility-Focused Staff Panel

This is the project I'm most proud to point at: a public-sector staff platform delivered for a European government institution, sitting inside a large, multi-team engineering organization where I owned the entire frontend codebase and the end-to-end test surface on my own.

The context. The product ran on Symfony on the backend and Vue.js on the frontend. It served internal staff workflows for a real public-sector audience — including visually impaired users, for whom keyboard-first navigation wasn't a feature, it was the only way they could do their job.

My ownership. Frontend and E2E testing were 100% mine. I shipped custom keyboard shortcuts, focus management, ARIA refinements and screen-reader-aware interaction patterns as the primary delivery, not as a checklist at the end of a sprint. I refactored the existing store-controller layer to make the UI behavior more predictable, modular and testable, and rebuilt the navigation model around real keyboard-first users rather than synthetic compliance.

The test surface. I treated the Nightwatch E2E suite as a first-class product surface, not a CI checkbox. I grew the coverage to lock down the critical user flows that visually impaired staff relied on every day, so accessibility regressions would fail CI before they ever reached a real user.

Why it matters to me. This wasn't a portfolio demo. It was software used by real public-sector employees, where a broken focus trap or a missing aria attribute meant someone couldn't get their work done. Building at that bar, for that audience, inside a large organization where my work carried real weight, is exactly the kind of engineering I want to keep doing.
